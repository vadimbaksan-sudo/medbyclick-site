## 1. Executive Summary

MBC is the utility token of the MedByClick telemedicine platform — a curated marketplace connecting patients worldwide with 40+ personally vetted medical specialists for complex and rare medical cases.

MBC provides concrete utility within the platform ecosystem: discounted payments for medical services, loyalty rewards for active users, access to premium features, and participation in platform governance. The token launches on BNB Chain (BEP-20) for low transaction costs, with planned expansion to Ethereum (ERC-20) as the ecosystem matures.

This document describes the token's role, mechanics, and launch strategy. MBC is a utility token — it is not an investment product and carries no guarantees regarding future value.

**Token overview:**
- **Name:** MedByClick Token
- **Symbol:** MBC
- **Standard:** BEP-20 (BNB Chain) / ERC-20 (Ethereum, future)
- **Total supply:** 100,000,000 MBC (fixed, non-mintable)
- **Launch price:** $0.01 USD
- **Launch platform:** PancakeSwap (BNB Chain)

## 2. Problem Statement

### 2.1 Pain Points in Telemedicine Payments

1. **High cross-border fees:** International card payments for medical consultations cost 3-7% in processing and currency conversion fees
2. **Payment delays:** Cross-border bank transfers take 3-7 business days, delaying care coordination
3. **No patient engagement incentives:** Traditional payment systems offer no mechanism to reward loyal patients or encourage platform participation
4. **Access barriers:** Patients in many countries lack reliable international payment options
5. **No patient voice:** Users have no structured way to influence platform development

### 2.2 How MBC Addresses These Problems

- **Lower fees:** BNB Chain transactions cost $0.05-0.20 vs. $15-50 for international transfers
- **Instant settlement:** On-chain payments confirm in seconds
- **Built-in rewards:** Smart contract automates loyalty token distribution
- **Global access:** Anyone with a smartphone can acquire and use MBC
- **On-chain governance:** Token holders can participate in platform decisions

## 3. Token Utility

MBC value is derived entirely from its use within the MedByClick platform. The following utility mechanisms create organic demand through platform activity, not speculation.

### 3.1 Service Payment Discount

Patients who pay with MBC receive a **15% discount** on platform services:

| Service | Standard Price | MBC Price | Patient Savings |
|---------|---------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Specialist Consultation | $500 | $425 (in MBC) | $75 |
| Case Review | $200 | $170 (in MBC) | $30 |
| Second Opinion | $350 | $297.50 (in MBC) | $52.50 |
| Care Coordination | $150 | $127.50 (in MBC) | $22.50 |

The discount is funded by the platform's savings on payment processing (no card processor fees). This creates a rational economic reason for patients to acquire and hold MBC — actual cost savings, not speculative upside.

### 3.2 Loyalty Rewards

Users earn MBC through platform engagement:

| Action | MBC Earned |
|--------|-----------|
| Complete a consultation | 50 MBC |
| Write a verified review | 25 MBC |
| Refer a friend (who completes booking) | 100 MBC |
| Complete patient profile | 10 MBC |
| Complete a MedEdu course | 30 MBC |

Annual rewards budget: 2,000,000 MBC (from Platform Rewards allocation), adjusted based on platform activity.

### 3.3 Membership Tiers

Holding MBC unlocks incremental platform benefits:

| Tier | Min. MBC Held | Benefits |
|------|--------------|----------|
| **Standard** | 0 | Base platform access |
| **Silver** | 500 | 5% additional discount, priority scheduling |
| **Gold** | 2,000 | 10% additional discount, dedicated coordinator, quarterly case review |
| **Platinum** | 10,000 | 15% additional discount, 24/7 direct line, annual health review |

Tiers require holding (not spending) MBC — this encourages long-term holding and reduces circulating supply naturally.

### 3.4 Platform Access Features

MBC unlocks specific platform capabilities not available to non-holders:

- **Priority queue:** MBC holders get faster specialist matching (24h vs. 72h standard)
- **Extended consultations:** Gold+ tiers receive 15-minute extension on video consultations
- **Medical record vault:** Encrypted cloud storage for medical documents (Silver+)
- **Specialist direct messaging:** Direct async messaging with specialists between appointments (Gold+)
- **Multi-language case summaries:** AI-generated case summaries in patient's native language (Platinum)

### 3.5 Specialist Incentive Program

Specialists on the platform can earn MBC bonuses:

- Maintaining 4.5+ rating: 200 MBC/month
- Publishing educational content: 50 MBC per article
- Participating in complex case reviews: 100 MBC per review
- Accepting MBC payments: 5% bonus on MBC-paid consultations

This creates a two-sided marketplace incentive — both patients and specialists benefit from the token economy.

### 3.6 Data Contribution Rewards

Patients who opt-in to anonymized data sharing for medical research earn MBC:

- Anonymous health survey completion: 20 MBC
- Clinical trial matching (opt-in): 50 MBC
- Treatment outcome feedback: 30 MBC

All data is anonymized and GDPR/HIPAA compliant. Participation is strictly voluntary.

### 3.7 Governance

MBC holders can participate in platform governance decisions:

- Vote on new module priorities
- Propose feature requests (minimum 500 MBC to submit)
- Vote on ecosystem fund allocations (minimum 100 MBC to vote)

Voting power is proportional to holdings. Governance is advisory in the early stage, transitioning to binding DAO votes as the community matures.

### 4.1 Supply Distribution

| Allocation | % | MBC Amount | Purpose | Release Schedule |
|-----------|---|-----------|---------|-----------------|

### 4.2 Why This Distribution

- **30% rewards** — largest allocation ensures long-term user incentive runway (5+ years of rewards)
- **25% ecosystem** — funds for real integrations with clinics, insurance, and health systems
- **15% team** — industry standard with long vesting to align team incentives with project longevity
- **10% liquidity** — sufficient for initial DEX pools on PancakeSwap
- **10% community** — small allocation prevents excessive initial selling pressure
- **10% reserve** — safety net, locked and multisig-controlled

### 4.3 Initial Listing

**Platform:** PancakeSwap v2 (BNB Chain)
**Pair:** MBC/USDT

**Initial liquidity calculation:**

```
Target starting price: $0.01 per MBC
USDT deposited: $5,000

AMM formula: price = USDT_in_pool / MBC_in_pool
$0.01 = $5,000 / MBC_amount
MBC_amount = 500,000 MBC

Initial pool: 500,000 MBC + 5,000 USDT
```

**Why $5,000 initial liquidity:**
- Appropriate for a pre-revenue startup — does not overcommit capital
- At $0.01/MBC, a $50 purchase moves price ~1% — reasonable for early-stage
- A $500 purchase moves price ~10% — natural price discovery
- Can be increased as platform revenue grows
- LP tokens locked for 12 months to prevent rug-pull concerns

### 4.4 Burn Mechanism

2% of MBC used for platform payments is burned (sent to dead address):

- Permanently reduces total supply over time
- Burn rate scales with platform usage — more activity = more deflation
- Creates long-term supply reduction without artificial mechanisms

*Example: If $100,000 in annual MBC payments, ~200,000 MBC burned/year at $0.01*

### 4.5 Anti-Whale Protection (Smart Contract Level)

| Parameter | Value | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Max transaction | 1% of supply (1,000,000 MBC) | Prevents single large dumps |
| Max wallet | 3% of supply (3,000,000 MBC) | Prevents excessive accumulation |
| Cooldown | 30 seconds between transactions | Prevents bot manipulation |

- Owner (team multisig) is exempt for operational needs (distributing rewards, seeding pools)
- Limits are adjustable and removable as token distribution matures
- Exemptions can be granted for verified partners (e.g., clinic payment wallets)

## 5. Technical Architecture

### 5.1 Smart Contract

```
Contract: MBCToken.sol
Base: OpenZeppelin v5.1 (ERC20 + ERC20Burnable + Ownable)
Solidity: ^0.8.20
Compiler: optimizer enabled (200 runs)

Features:
  - Fixed 100M supply, minted to owner at deploy
  - Burnable (any holder can burn their tokens)
  - Pausable (emergency freeze by owner)
  - Anti-whale (maxTx, maxWallet, cooldown)
  - Exempt addresses (owner, verified partners)
  - Adjustable limits (setLimits, removeLimits)
```

### 5.2 Network Strategy

**Phase 1 (Launch) — BNB Chain:**
- Primary network for all operations
- PancakeSwap liquidity pool (MBC/USDT)
- Low gas ($0.05-0.20) makes MBC practical for everyday payments
- Target users: patients making $10-500 transactions

**Phase 2 (Expansion) — Ethereum:**
- Deploy same contract on Ethereum mainnet
- Uniswap liquidity pool (MBC/USDT, MBC/ETH)
- Target: institutional partners, larger transactions
- Cross-chain bridge between BNB Chain and Ethereum

**Rationale for BNB Chain first:**
- Gas fees 100-500x lower than Ethereum
- Faster block times (3s vs. 12s)
- Lower barrier to entry for patients unfamiliar with crypto
- PancakeSwap has higher retail user accessibility
- Ethereum expansion planned when justified by transaction volume

### 5.3 Security Measures

- **OpenZeppelin contracts:** Battle-tested, widely audited standard library
- **No mint function:** Total supply is permanently fixed — impossible to inflate
- **Multisig ownership:** Contract ownership will be transferred to Gnosis Safe (2-of-3 signers)
- **LP lock:** Liquidity pool tokens locked for 12 months via Team.Finance or equivalent
- **Anti-whale:** On-chain limits prevent market manipulation
- **Open source:** Contract code is publicly verifiable on BscScan/Etherscan

## 7. Utility Analysis: Why MBC Demand Should Grow With Platform Usage

The core question for any utility token: **will people actually need to buy and hold this token?**

MBC is designed so that demand is driven by platform usage, not speculation:

### 7.1 Demand Drivers (Why Users Acquire MBC)

1. **Payment discount (15%)** — rational economic incentive. A patient paying $500/consultation saves $75 by using MBC. Over 4 consultations/year = $300 savings. This alone justifies acquiring MBC.

2. **Tier benefits** — holding 500-10,000 MBC unlocks real features (priority scheduling, direct messaging, dedicated coordinator). These have tangible value for patients with complex cases.

3. **Rewards earning** — patients earn MBC through platform activity and can use earned tokens for future payments, creating a self-reinforcing cycle.

4. **Specialist participation** — specialists earn MBC bonuses, creating demand on the supply side of the marketplace too.

### 7.2 Supply Reduction Mechanisms (Why MBC Becomes Scarcer)

1. **Burn on payment** — 2% of every MBC payment is permanently destroyed
2. **Tier holding** — users hold MBC for tier benefits, reducing circulating supply
3. **Fixed supply** — no new MBC can ever be minted
4. **Staking (Phase 4)** — locked tokens for yield further reduces liquid supply

### 7.3 Potential Risks to Demand

- **Low platform adoption** — if MedByClick doesn't grow, MBC utility is limited
- **Regulatory changes** — utility token regulation is evolving
- **Competition** — other telemedicine platforms could launch competing tokens
- **User preference** — some users may prefer traditional payment despite discount

These risks are inherent to any early-stage project. MBC's value proposition depends on MedByClick platform success.

### 7.4 Additional Utility Opportunities (Future Consideration)

To strengthen long-term demand beyond the initial utility model:

1. **Medical NFT Credentials** — verified health records as NFTs, requiring MBC to mint
2. **Specialist Booking Deposits** — refundable MBC deposits for appointment booking (reduces no-shows)
3. **Cross-Platform Health Passport** — MBC-powered portable medical profile usable across partner clinics
4. **Telemedicine API Access** — third-party developers pay MBC to access MedByClick APIs
5. **Health Data Marketplace** — anonymized, patient-consented data access for researchers, paid in MBC
6. **Emergency Medical Fund** — community pool where MBC holders contribute to cover emergency cases for underserved patients
